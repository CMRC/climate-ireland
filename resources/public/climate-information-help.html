Instructions for Using Tools and Data

Projected Climatic Data

Climate projection data employed includes those produced by McGrath et al. (2008), Sweeney et al. (2008) and Nolan et al. (2012a; 2012b)

Maps

The map to the left shows climate model projections for selected climate variables relative to the baseline period (1961-1990)

Users can choose to display projected changes for a variety of climatic variables, at a variety of temporal and spatial scales.

Climate projection data can be selected by the user according to season - Summer (June, July, August), Autumn (September, October, November), Winter (December, January, February), Spring (March, April, May) - or on an annual basis.

There are 5 emissions scenarios to choose from: A2, high emissions; A1B, fairly high emissions; ensemble, a multi-model unweighted average value calculated from all projections and emissions scenarios.

Graphs

Using the map tool, users can select an area of interest and further examine the projected climatic information for this area using the graph on the right

For the selected variable, season and area of interest, the boxplot to the right shows the range of values projected for each decade calculated for all available scenarios.

For each decade, the mean of all projections is shown, as well as the maximum and minimum values.

The thick bar represents the range over which half all of the projected values lie.
